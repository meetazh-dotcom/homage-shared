How Your Pay Is Calculated
All components appear separately on your monthly payslip
💲
Base Salary
Your contracted monthly base pay. This is the amount used to calculate OT rates and statutory entitlements.
🚑
Fixed Allowances
Fixed monthly transport allowance as stated in your contract. Does not change based on hours worked. Not convertible to cash.
⏰
Overtime (OT)
Hours beyond 44/week are paid at 1.5× your basic hourly rate. OT must be pre-approved. Monthly cap: 72 hours (MOM regulation).
🕑
Your Total Hours
Care hours (clocked via app) + auto-calculated travel time = your total paid hours. You don’t submit travel time separately — Homage calculates it.
For HPC cases: Review the InterAI Care Assessment Report for full clinical context.

Monthly payout — always by the 7th of the month after the salary period
📅 First Salary — Paid the Following Month
Covers the month you start work. Paid by the 7th calendar day of the following month.

Your salary in the first month is pro-rated based on your actual start date. You are paid only for the days you worked.

Your payslip shows each component separately: base salary, allowances, OT, and travel time. If the total looks different from expected, compare all components — not just base salary.

Urgent Leave — Emergencies Only
This leave type exists for unforeseen situations where you truly cannot work. It is not for convenience.
🚨
What Qualifies
Genuine emergencies only — situations that could not have been anticipated. It should not be used for convenience or non-emergency personal matters.

Requests are reviewed and approved on a case-by-case basis. Supporting documents must be submitted promptly where required.
💬
How to Report
WhatsApp the SC team immediately once you know you cannot work.

If you are rostered on upcoming days, you must update the SC team on whether you are fit to return within 2 hours of reporting urgent leave. This is required to avoid last-minute disruption to visits.
📅
Leave Deduction
Urgent leave exceeding half a day will be deducted from your AL balance, subject to approval.

If no valid leave balance is available, it may be treated as unpaid leave, subject to Homage review.
☝
Why It Matters
Taking urgent leave directly impacts Care Recipients who depend on you. Every absence requires the team to arrange last-minute coverage.

Late updates or failure to confirm availability for upcoming rostered days may be escalated for review.

Module A — Home Cleaning Visits (1 of 2)
Light and heavy housekeeping tasks covered during structured home cleaning visits
☀️ Light Housekeeping
● Sweep, vacuum, and mop accessible floor areas in living areas, bedrooms, and toilets.
● Light dusting and wiping of reachable surfaces.
● Make or refresh beds, including bedsheet changes.
● Clean toilet bowls, wash basins, and scrub bathroom floors.
● Remove rubbish, replace bin liners, and tidy visible clutter.
● Does not include window cleaning, fan cleaning, or moving furniture.
🧹 Heavy Housekeeping
● Clean interior window glass, frames, tracks, and accessible grilles.
● Wipe fan blades and covers from a safe, reachable height.
● Mandatory: use ladders with safety lock mechanisms only. Standing on chairs or unstable surfaces is strictly not allowed.
● Typically combined within a 4-hour session per month.

Time-Sensitive Medical Incident — Recognise It Immediately
When a care recipient shows signs of a life-threatening emergency
⚠ Examples of Medical Emergencies
• Loss of consciousness
• Chest pain
• Breathing difficulties
• Severe fall or injury
• Seizures
• Choking
• Severe bleeding
• Stroke signs
• Hypoglycaemia (severe)
• Unresponsiveness
Key Principle
Do not wait. Do not second-guess.
Act first, report after.
If the person is in immediate danger, your priority is their safety. Everything else — documentation, process, notification — comes after the emergency is handled.
©2026 Homage Co Pte Ltd. All rights reserved. Confidential & for internal use only.
