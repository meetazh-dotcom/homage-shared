How Your Pay Is Calculated
All components appear separately on your monthly payslip
💲
Base Salary
Your contracted monthly base pay. This is the amount used to calculate OT rates and statutory entitlements.
🚑
Fixed Allowances
Fixed monthly transport allowance as stated in your contract. Does not change based on hours worked. Not convertible to cash.
⏰
Overtime (OT)
Hours beyond 44/week are paid at 1.5× your basic hourly rate. OT must be pre-approved. Monthly cap: 72 hours (MOM regulation).
🕑
Your Total Hours
Care hours (clocked via app) + auto-calculated travel time = your total paid hours. You don’t submit travel time separately — Homage calculates it.

Your Salary Is Paid by the 7th of the Following Month
Monthly payout — always by the 7th of the month after the salary period
📅 First Salary — Paid the Following Month
Covers the month you start work. Paid by the 7th calendar day of the following month.

Example: Start in May → May salary paid by 7 June.
🕑 Subsequent Salaries
All following months paid by the 7th of the month after the salary period.

June salary → paid by 7 July. And so on.
✅ First Month Pro-Ration
Your salary in the first month is pro-rated based on your actual start date. You are paid only for the days you worked.
📋 Reading Your Payslip
Your payslip shows each component separately: base salary, allowances, OT, and travel time. If the total looks different from expected, compare all components — not just base salary.

If You Are Sick — Report It and Get an MC
No MC = no paid sick leave. Follow these steps every time.
1
Report Immediately
WhatsApp the Service Coordination team as soon as you know you cannot work. Do not wait until your shift starts.
Same day — before your shift
2
See a Doctor & Get an MC
Visit a government doctor, MOM-approved practitioner, or use an approved teleconsult. The MC must clearly state the dates you are unfit for work.
Same day or next working day
3
Submit MC to People Team
Send a photo or scan of your MC to the People Team (people@homage.co). Keep the original — you may be asked to produce it.
Within 1 working day of your MC date
ⓘ Important: A valid MC must state the exact dates you are unfit for work. Vague notes or receipts without dates are not accepted. Without a valid MC, your absence is recorded as unpaid leave.

Housekeeping Scope & Boundaries
Know exactly what is in scope — and what is not
🗒 Light Housekeeping (Personal Care Visits)
CR’s bedroom only: sweep, mop, vacuum, dust, change sheets. Bathroom: spray down. Kitchen: stove area & utensils if used. Laundry: max 10 items, washing machine only. No hand-washing.
🏠 Module A — Home Cleaning Visits
Light: Sweep, mop, dust all accessible areas, toilet cleaning, bed changes, rubbish removal.
Heavy: Window glass, fan blades, grilles — ladders with safety lock only. Typically 4h/month.
Laundry: Machine or laundromat.
🚫 Out of Scope — Never Do These
• Window cleaning, fan cleaning (light HK)
• Moving furniture or heavy objects
• Mopping the entire home
• Cleaning electronics (fans, appliances)
• Scrubbing bathroom floors (light HK)
• Standing on chairs or unstable surfaces
• Hand-washing of any clothing
• Medical or nursing procedures
• Financial advice or handling money
ⓘ Rule: If a client requests something outside your scope, record it in your Visit Summary and escalate to SC.

Time-Sensitive Medical Incident — Recognise It Immediately
When a care recipient shows signs of a life-threatening emergency
⚠ Examples of Medical Emergencies
• Loss of consciousness
• Chest pain
• Breathing difficulties
• Severe fall or injury
• Seizures
• Choking
• Severe bleeding
• Stroke signs
• Hypoglycaemia (severe)
• Unresponsiveness
Key Principle
Do not wait. Do not second-guess.
Act first, report after.
If the person is in immediate danger, your priority is their safety. Everything else — documentation, process, notification — comes after the emergency is handled.
©2026 Homage Co Pte Ltd. All rights reserved. Confidential & for internal use only.
Medical Emergency — Step-by-Step Actions
Follow these steps in order. Speed matters.
1
If NOK/CO is present — inform them immediately, advise to call 995
2
If NOK/CO is absent — call 995 yourself immediately
3
Stay on-site. Perform CPR if trained and needed.
4
Notify SC via WhatsApp as soon as you can
5
Document all events chronologically in your Visit Summary

Other Incidents — Report to SC Without Delay
Any event causing harm, distress, or risk that does not require 995 — report immediately, do not wait until after the visit
Incident TypeWhat To Do
Fall / minor injuryHelp CR to safety. Check for pain. Notify SC immediately. If serious (head strike, unconscious, heavy bleeding) → treat as medical emergency.
Verbal abuse or threatening behaviourStay calm. Remove yourself if at risk of physical harm. Contact SC. Do not re-enter until instructed.
Physical assault or inappropriate sexual conductRemove yourself immediately. Contact SC. Call 995 if there is serious injury.
TheftDo not confront. Contact SC immediately. Preserve the scene — do not touch or move items.
Suspected neglect or abuseDocument observations only. Report to SC immediately. Do not confront or accuse.
Your safety first. When in doubt, escalate. Preserve all evidence.
